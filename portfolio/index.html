I am a 22 year old artist residing in Brooklyn exploring the magic of the internet. I grew up in Connecticut and moved to New York in 2017 to pursue a BFA in Design and Tech and a BA in Screen Studies from The New School. Iâ€™m a programmer working with physical computation, internet art, and experimental video work. Being part of the first generation of adolescents growing up with social media my work is very influenced by my early internet experiences. Growing up I often stayed up all night in chatrooms, on Tumblr, or interacting with friends I made via the internet. My aim with my work is to channel that nostalgia we all felt exploring the internet for the first time alone and use it to educate or question ideas about living in a digital world.


I make sites and physical artworks that explore ideas and politics around tech. I am very drawn to net art design, maximalism, and online satire pieces. My video work revolves around notions of radical honesty online and how we can use digital interfaces to tell these stories, not only in presenting but involving them as a medium. I try to convey vulnerability and intimacy on the internet by employing auto ethnographic research when working on my projects. I am very drawn to research on online communities and how they can be empowering for internet users.


An important part of my mission is also creating tools for sex workers, the LGBTQ community, and internet explorers. Creating spaces of care online for communities that I am part of is helping me transform the internet landscape into a safer space while also spreading knowledge. I work on pieces that are important to me and involve the communities that I am a part of.


In the future I want to have my own fine art practice and also be a teacher to help educate other young artists on how to code. I'm really invested in open source and open knowledge, helping to encourage the potential we all have to make pieces online.
